All Our Events

The Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC), Sri Venkateswara College organizes a diverse range of inclusive events throughout the year to empower differently-abled students, foster inclusivity, and build community. From educational and adventure trips to workshops, seminars, and annual celebrations, each event is designed to engage students, volunteers, and PwD members in meaningful and enjoyable ways.

Helen Keller Day: Annual Webinar and Quiz

One of our flagship events, the Helen Keller Day Webinar, is organized virtually every year to celebrate the spirit of resilience and determination. This event brings together inspirational speakers and achievers from diverse fields, offering students a chance to learn from their journeys. In the past, we have had the honor of hosting Padma Shri awardee Shekhar Naik [former captain of the Indian Blind Cricket Team, Mr. Manu Garg (AIR 91, UPSC CSE 2024), Ms. Himani Bundela, the winner of the popular television show Kaun Banega Crorepati, Mr. Dipendra Manocha [founder of Saksham Trust] who shared their story of overcoming challenges and achieving excellence. Through these webinars, students are encouraged to embrace challenges with confidence and to believe that limitations can never define one’s potential. Along with the webinar, EOC also conducts an interactive quiz, offering participants the chance to win exciting cash prizes.

Educational Trips

Educational trips are a core part of the Equal Opportunity Cell’ s annualactivities. These visits are designed to provide exposure, interaction, and learning opportunities forboth our volunteers and PwD members. In the past we have visited institutions such as AkhilBhartiya Netrahin Sangh (Blind School), All India Confederation of the Blinds, Gurugram, etc. Thistrip enables our volunteers to interact closely with PwD students and observe how they excelacademically and personally in an environment tailored to their needs. These annual educationaltrips have become a signature tradition of EOC, serving as a bridge for knowledge exchange and as aplatform to cultivate empathy, awareness, and inclusivity among students.

Navigation & Mobility Workshop

The Navigation & Mobility Workshop was launched with theaim of empowering visually impaired students to move independently within the college campus.The workshop focused on familiarizing students with key areas such as classrooms, the library, thecanteen, and other essential facilities. In the initial phase, EOC volunteers actively guided visuallyimpaired peers through every corner of the campus, ensuring that they developed a sense of directionand confidence. The workshop has proved to be a remarkable success, as it not only fulfilled its goal of enhancing independent mobility but also strengthened the bond between volunteers and PwD students.

Nukkad Natak

On 8th December 2023, the Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC) observed the International Week of Disability under the aegis of the Ministry of Social Justice. As part of this initiative, the Navjyoti Foundation visited Sri Venkateswara College and conducted an engaging Nukkad Natak (street play) to raise awareness about the challenges faced by differently-abled individuals. Additionally, in collaboration with Aaroh NGO, EOC organized a Disability Awareness Nukkad Natak, offering students a unique and immersive learning experience. The event not only highlighted the importance of inclusivity and empathy but also encouraged active participation and reflection among the audience.

Abhyudaya Seminar

Every year, the Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC) organizes अभ्युदय, a seminar aimed at providing guidance and inspiration to differently-abled students. We have hosted speakers like Saloni Khanna ma’am, a renowned expert known for conducting mock interviews for UPSC CSE at a coaching Institute and Dr. Vijendra Chauhan who is an associate professor at Zakir Hussain College, University of Delhi and is one of most prominent conductors of mock interviews for UPSC 6. 7. 8. CSE at a coaching Institute. The seminar offers invaluable insights into overcoming challenges, planning preparation, and achieving success in competitive exams. Held annually, अभ्युदय has become a significant platform for mentorship, guidance, and empowerment, benefiting differently-abled students and all participants.

Adventure Trips

Every year, the Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC) organizes adventure trips to foster camaraderie, confidence, and inclusvity among differently-abled students and volunteers. We have arranged fun-filled trips to Madhavgarh Farms, Vishalgarh farms, rangmanch farms where PwD students and volunteers participated with great enthusiasm. The activities encouraged teamwork, outdoor engagement, and created lasting memories for all participants. These trips prove to be memorable experiences, strengthening the bonds between volunteers and PwD students while providing opportunities for personal growth and enjoyment.

Lahar – The Annual Fest

The annual fest: Every year, the Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC) hosts “Lahar”, a vibrant two-day annual fest that brings together differently-abled students and volunteers from across colleges. It is a two-day event, dedicated to our goal of connecting differently abled with mainstream society by instilling a feeling of belonging and confidence in everyone through various competitions and Sport Activities. Designed to celebrate talent, inclusivity, and community spirit, Lahar offers a platform for students to showcase their skills, engage in friendly competition, and build lasting connections. The fest features a variety of exciting events and performances, such as singing competitions (Swar Sangam), poetry recitals (Lafz-e-Shama), treasure hunts (Talaash), quizzes (QuizAbility), and sports activities. The celebration is concluded with a DJ evening. Lahar is more than just a fest- it is a platform for empowerment. All competitions and events organized during Lahar by the Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC) feature exciting cash prizes for winners.

Workshops

The Equal Opportunity Cell (EOC) regularly organizes workshops to enhance skills and knowledge among differently-abled students and volunteers. These workshops focus on creating inclusive learning environments, introducing participants to innovative tools, and fostering personal and academic growth. For example, on 22 March 2025, EOC in collaboration with the SVC Library and Saksham NGO conducted sessions on assistive technologies for visually impaired students, where participants learnt to navigate academics with greater ease and confidence. In the past we have also organized “Sashaktikaran workshop” for skill development and job orientation programmes for the differently abled students. Through such workshops, EOC aims to promote accessibility, technological literacy, and independence, while also providing a platform for volunteers and students to engage, learn and grow together
